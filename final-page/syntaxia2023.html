  • Code Wars

    A group coding and debugging event where the participants are given challenging problems and are judged by their abilities to write efficient, error free and well documented code.

  • BrainWave Byte

    A group event where the participants are tested on their knowledge on a wide range of topics from computer hardware to cloud architectures, with various rounds like buzzer rounds, rapid fire and so on.

  • Visionary Voice

    This event is an opportunity for students to showcase their knowledge and share their thoughts on the latest trends and advancements in the field of IT. It's a platform for students to learn and grow by listening to the experiences and ideas of their peers. Rules: The teams will have 15 minutes for presentations, followed by a question-and-answer session, The time limit should be strictly followed, and extension may lead to deduction of marks, Decision of the judges will be final.

  • Project Prodigy

    This event is an opportunity for IT students to showcase their ability to plan, organize, and execute a project from start to finish. Participants will display their ability to manage time, resources and handle stress during this event.

  • TopNotch Design

    This competition is an opportunity for students to demonstrate their ability to create visually appealing and user-friendly interfaces using CSS. Participants will be given a design brief and will be judged on their ability to create a responsive and visually appealing layout, use of CSS effects and features and their attention to detail.

  • Tech-Tangle

    This event is a potpourri of fun activities all related to IT. With a mix of serious and light-hearted activities, it's an opportunity for students to showcase their tech skills in a fun and relaxed atmosphere. It's a great way to bond with your peers and learn something new in the process.

  • WereWolves within

    It is fun and exciting event where participants will have to solve a series of IT-related puzzles and riddles to find the hidden "treasure". Along the way, participants will be testing their knowledge in different IT-related areas, such as programming languages, hardware, and cybersecurity.
